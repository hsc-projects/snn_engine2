Sigmoidal Connection Probability#

Find \(\alpha_{g, \tau}, \beta_{g, \tau}\) and \(\gamma_{g, \tau}\) such that

\[\sum_{d \in \{ 0, ..., D-1\}} \Big[P(d, \alpha_{g, \tau}, \beta_{g, \tau}, \gamma_{g, \tau}) \quad * \quad \text{TypedNeuronCountD}_D[d, g, \tau]\Big] \approx S\]

for each group \(g \in \{0, ..., G -1\}\) and neuron-type \(\tau \in \{1, 2\}\).

Starting values:

\[\begin{split}\begin{eqnarray} \alpha_{inh} &=& 4, \\ \alpha_{exc} &=& 2, \\ \beta_{inh} &=& 1 + \frac{D}{3}, \\ \beta_{exc} &=& 1, \\ \gamma_{inh} &=& 0.01, \\ \gamma_{exc} &=& 0.05. \end{eqnarray}\end{split}\]
\[\text{sig}(d, \alpha) = 1 - \frac{1}{1 + e^{- \alpha d + 1}}\]
\[P(d, \alpha, \beta) = \min \bigg(1, \frac{\beta}{D} \Big(\text{sig}(d, \alpha) + \gamma \big(1-\frac{d^2}{D^2}\big)\Big)\bigg)\]
